News 19 November 2024

Fluctuations in blood pressure over time may be linked to higher sudden cardiac death risk

Middle-aged adults with fluctuating blood pressure face a significantly higher risk of sudden cardiac death, with up to 70% greater risk for those with the most variability, according to a large study presented at the American Heart Association's Scientific Sessions. The study tracked 23,499 participants over 30 years, showing that even a 1 mmHg increase in systolic variability raised the risk by 3%.

By : Laura Williamson
