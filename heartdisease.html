The Risk Factors of Heart Disease


Risk factors for heart disease are conditions or behaviors that increase the likelihood of developing cardiovascular issues like coronary artery disease or heart attack. Non-modifiable factors include age, gender, family history, and ethnicity. Modifiable factors are linked to lifestyle and health, such as smoking, unhealthy diets, physical inactivity, excessive alcohol consumption, and medical conditions like high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, and obesity.

Non-Modifiedable


Non-modifiable risk factors are things you cannot change, such as your age, gender, family history, and genetics. While you can't control these, it's still important to be aware of them, as they can influence your risk of heart disease.

  • Family History

    A family history of heart disease, especially if immediate family members (like parents or siblings) developed heart conditions at a young age, can increase one’s risk due to shared genetics and possibly similar lifestyle factors.

  • Age Category

    As people age, the risk of heart disease increases. Aging can lead to changes in the heart and blood vessels, such as stiffening of arteries, which contributes to a higher risk of cardiovascular conditions.

  • Gender

    Men generally have a higher risk of heart disease at a younger age, while women’s risk increases and often catches up after menopause due to the reduction in protective estrogen levels.
